Sources for Data

**Baumsnow**
All data files retrieved from paper “Did Highways Cause Suburbanization?“ at Nate Baumsnow’s website at 
https://sites.google.com/site/baumsnow/research

**CBD Data***
CBD Codes - Matt Holian, Google Earth, 
http://mattholian.blogspot.com/2013/05/central-business-district-geocodes.html

**CPI**
Bureau of Labor Statistics, Databases, Tables & Calculators by Subject, Inflation & Prices, https://www.bls.gov/data/

**Crosswalks**
State fips codes - Census, FIPS Codes for the States and the District of Columbia, 
https://www.census.gov/geo/reference/ansi_statetables.html

SMA to County Crosswalk 1950 - Census, STANDARD METROPOLITAN AREAS (SMAs) AND COMPONENTS, 1950, WITH FIPS CODES, 
https://www.census.gov/population/estimates/metro-city/50mfips.txt

Urban Area to county/CBSA crosswalk 2010 - Census, Urban Area 2010 relationship files, 
https://www.census.gov/geo/maps-data/data/ua_rel_download.html

CBSA to principal cities crosswalk 2010 - Census, Historical Delineation Files, 
https://www2.census.gov/programs-surveys/metro-micro/geographies/reference-files/2009/historical-delineation-files/list2.txt

MSA Fips codes 2000 - Census, Historical Delineation files, 
https://www2.census.gov/programs-surveys/metro-micro/geographies/reference-files/1999/historical-delineation-files/99mfips.txt

County changes 2000 to 2010 - Census, Substantial Changes to Counties and County Equivalent Entities: 1970-Present: 2000 tab
https://www.census.gov/geo/reference/county-changes.html

Longitudinal Tract Data Base 1970 to 2010 - Brown University, 
https://s4.ad.brown.edu/Projects/Diversity/Researcher/LTDB.htm 

**Highway Distances**
Distances from centroid of census tracts (2010 definitions) to the nearest highway line. Calculated using GIS files of digitized 1947 highway plan map and yellow book images retrieved from 
https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Interstate_Highway_plan_August_2,_1947_big_text.jpg
https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:1955_Yellow_Book_maps  
See (code/gis R code/calcdist_highwaytract.R)

**Lee and Lin data**
All data files retrieved from supplemental data section of paper “Natural Amenities, Neighbourhood Dynamics, and Persistence in the Spatial Distribution of Income” by Sanghoon Lee  Jeffrey Lin at ReStud link 
https://academic.oup.com/restud/advance-article/doi/10.1093/restud/rdx018/3091867 

**NHGIS**
All data files retrieved from tables at 
https://www.nhgis.org/

**NLSY**
All data files retrieved from 
http://www.nlsinfo.org/web-investigator/. 
Geocode files (not included) only given by application. See 
https://www.bls.gov/nls/nlsfaqs.htm#anch25

**UCR data**
Uniform crime reports data for cities in 1950 from archive.org (courtesy of Boston Public Library) at 
https://archive.org/details/uniformcrimerepo1950unit

Data on population of urbanized areas in 1950. Census Bureau 1950 report Vol 1. Number of Inhabitants, retrieved from https://www2.census.gov/library/publications/decennial/1950/population-volume-2/21983999v2p1ch2.pdf 

The above two measures are used to calculate a crime rate (# crimes per 100,000 persons)
